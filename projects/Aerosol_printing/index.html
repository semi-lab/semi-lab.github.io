CSAM offers unique advantages, enabling the production of complex, high-performance components with enhanced durability and reduced material waste. Its remarkable features have a profound impact on industries by revolutionizing surface manufacturing and fostering sustainable practices.
We aim to uncover process-structure-property relationships of the CSAM to unlock its unique potential in various industries. For these endeavors, we thoroughly investigate CSAM, from in-flight to upon-impact characteristics of the particles, as well as microstructural characterization. Our overarching objective is to explore this emerging AM technique to create functional and self-powered smart surfaces for the realm of Internet of Things (IoT).

Our current directions include:
  • Multifield modeling of CSAM.
  • Design and development of smart and self-powered surfaces for IoT.
  • Sustainable repairing of printed electronics
